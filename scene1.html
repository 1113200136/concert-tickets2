SCENE 1

☕ 24 Hours Before the Sale

Lily prepares her ticketing account — the night before the big day.

👤
Who
Lily, Zoe & Maya
🕐
When & Where
24h before ·
a café
⏱
Length
~1.5 minutes
🎯 Learning Aim: To know the vocabulary needed to register on a ticketing website
🎬

Storyboard

LILY: The tickets go on sale tomorrow at noon! What if the Wi-Fi is slow? What if they sell out in 30 seconds?!
ZOE: Relax. I'll open the website at 11:55 and buy them. Easy.
LILY: ZOE. This is Jay Chou. This is not "just a website."
MAYA: WRONG! Both of you. If you wait until tomorrow, you will FAIL.
ZOE: What are we supposed to do right now? The sale isn't until tomorrow!
MAYA: Right now you register an ACCOUNT. Tonight. Not tomorrow morning. TONIGHT.
LILY: I think I made one last year but I forgot the email…
MAYA: Find it. Then set a strong PASSWORD – not your birthday, not your pet's name.
ZOE: My password is my birthday…
MAYA: Change it. Now. Then VERIFY your account with your phone number. The website sends an SMS code. This proves you're human, not a bot.
ZOE: Why does all this matter so much? It's just an account.
MAYA: Because this is a real-name show. Your name must match your ID card exactly. One wrong character and the staff turns you away at the door. You paid, you waited, you go home. No concert.
LILY: That actually happens?!
MAYA: Every single show. And save your payment method tonight. Tomorrow you'll have fifteen seconds to buy before someone grabs your spot. Fifteen. Seconds.
LILY: My phone! I never finished verifying my number! Where is it?!
MAYA: Amateurs.
📖
Review

Key Vocabulary

🔒
